      Deep dive

      Per-program detail

      Background How Canadian med school admissions work +

      Canadian medical schools do not require a specific undergraduate major — any completed bachelor's degree is eligible, though most schools require specific prerequisite courses. Requirements vary significantly: some schools (notably McMaster MD and UofT MD) have no specific course prerequisites at all, while others require organic chemistry, biochemistry, physics, statistics, and English.

      Not all schools require the MCAT (McGill dropped it for Canadian applicants; McMaster uses only the CARS section). Despite this flexibility, certain programs are widely recognized as premier pre-med pathways because of their curriculum alignment with the MCAT, their research infrastructure, and their track record of placing graduates into medical school.

      Appendix

      Dataset & methodology

      What this analysis is built on

      Every year, students in the r/OntarioUniversities and r/AlbertaGrade12s Reddit communities voluntarily report their university application outcomes — offers, rejections, deferrals, and waitlists — via public Google Forms. Each submission includes the student's top-6 average, the program they applied to, the decision they received, their province of residence, and (in later cycles) supplementary application details.

      This dashboard ingests, normalizes, and analyzes four cycles of these crowdsourced reports (2022–2023 through 2025–2026), covering a combined 8,600+ individual application outcomes across 27 tracked programs. The analysis focuses on Justin's Tier-1 and Tier-2 target programs, computing per-program accepted-average distributions, out-of-province subsets, year-over-year trends, and a percentile-based placement verdict calibrated to Justin's projected Grade 12 top-6 average.

      Application requirements (deadlines, supplementary apps, prerequisites, fees) are curated separately from each university's official admissions pages — not from the Reddit data. These are stored in config/requirements.yaml with per-entry confidence levels and source URLs.

      Key limitations to keep in mind

      1

      Severe self-selection bias

      ~93–99% of Reddit reports are acceptances. Students who get rejected rarely post. All "accepted average" numbers are floor estimates for who gets in — not 50/50 cutoff lines.

      2

      Small out-of-province samples

      OOP reports range from 4–12 per Tier-1 program across all cycles. The McMaster BHSc OOP floor (97.8%, n=5) is flagged as directional only — one data point could shift it by several percentage points.

      3

      Reddit population ≠ all applicants

      The dataset captures a self-selected slice of the applicant pool — predominantly Ontario high-school students active on r/OntarioUniversities. Alberta, BC, and international applicants are underrepresented.

      4

      Schema drift across cycles

      The 4 Google Forms used different question sets (10 fields in 2022–23 → 21 in 2023–24 → 15 in 2024–25/25–26). All are normalized to a canonical schema; see the QA report for mapping coverage.
